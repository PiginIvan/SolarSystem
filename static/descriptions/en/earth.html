General Characteristics and Atmosphere

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known place with life. Its atmosphere is 78% nitrogen and 21% oxygen, which allows living organisms to exist. The atmosphere protects the planet from ultraviolet radiation and maintains a stable temperature.

Water, Land, and Climate

Earth is covered by water 71% of the time and land 29%. The climate varies from hot equatorial regions to cold polar zones, contributing to the diversity of natural ecosystems.

Rotation Axis, Orbital Movement, and the Moon

Earth rotates around its axis in 24 hours, creating a day and night cycle. The orbital period takes 365.25 days. The Moon stabilizes the climate and causes tides on Earth.

Ecosystems and Biodiversity

Earth supports a rich diversity of life, from tropical forests to deserts, ensuring the stability of the biosphere.
