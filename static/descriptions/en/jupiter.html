General Characteristics and Atmosphere

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System, mostly composed of hydrogen and helium. Its atmosphere contains clouds of ammonia ice, methane, and water vapor. Jupiter does not have a solid surface, as it consists of gas and liquid.

Climate and Atmosphere

Jupiter has powerful atmospheric vortices, including the famous Great Red Spot — a massive storm that has been raging for hundreds of years. Its atmosphere is primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, and the temperature at cloud level is around -145°C.

Rotation Axis and Orbital Movement

Jupiter completes a rotation around its axis in 9 hours 55 minutes, making it the fastest rotating planet. Its orbital period is 11.86 years. The planet is located about 778.5 million km from the Sun.

Moons and Rings

Jupiter has over 70 known moons, including the largest moon in the Solar System — Ganymede. The planet also has rings, which are difficult to see due to their low brightness.
